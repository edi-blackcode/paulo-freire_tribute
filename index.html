Paulo Freire

The best Brazilian educator - one of the creaters of critical pedagogy.

Here's a time line of Paulo Freire:

  • 1921 - Paulo Reglus Neves Freire was born on September 19, 1921 in Recife, capital of the Brazilian state of Pernambuco.
  • 1929 - His family was part of the middle class, but Paulo Freire experienced childhood poverty and hunger during the depression of 1929, an experience that would lead him to care about the poorest and help him build his revolutionary method of literacy.
  • 1943 - Paulo Freire enters the Recife Faculty of Law. At that time, law school was the only alternative in the area of ​​humanities. During this period, he met primary school teacher Elza Maia Costa Oliveira.
  • 1944 - Paulo Freire enters the Recife Faculty of Law. At that time, law school was the only alternative in the area of ​​humanities. During this period, he met primary school teacher Elza Maia Costa Oliveira.
  • 1946 - Paulo Freire assumes the position of Director of the Department of Education and Culture of the Social Service, where he came into contact with the issue of adult/worker education and realized the need to carry out work aimed at literacy.
  • 1961 - In 1961, he became director of the Department of Cultural Extensions at the University of Recife and, in the same year, together with his team, he carried out the first experiences of popular literacy that would lead to the creation of the Paulo Freire Method.
  • 1963 - His team was responsible for teaching 300 cane cutters to read and write in just 45 days.
  • 1964 - Months after the implementation of this education plan, the military coup ended this work. Freire was imprisoned as a traitor for 70 days.
  • 1967 - During his exile, Paulo Freire passed through Bolivia and worked in Chile for five years for the Christian Democracy Agrarian Reform Movement and the Food and Agriculture Organization of the United Nations.
  • 1968 - In the previous year, he had completed the writing of his most famous book, Pedagogy of the Oppressed, which was published in several languages ​​such as Spanish, English (in 1970) and even Hebrew (in 1981).
  • 1969 - Freire was invited to be a visiting professor at Harvard University in 1969.
  • 1979 - With Amnesty in 1979 Freire was able to return to Brazil, but he only did so in 1980.
  • 1981 - He was a member of the Workers' Party, having been President of the 1st Executive Board of the Wilson Pinheiro Foundation, a party support foundation established by the PT in 1981 (predecessor of the Perseu Abramo Foundation).
  • 1989 - Freire was appointed Secretary of Education for the city of São Paulo. He held that position from 1989 to 1991.
  • 1991 - In 1991, the Paulo Freire Institute was created in São Paulo to expand and elaborate on its theories of popular education. The institute already has projects in many countries and is based at the School of Education and Information Studies at the University of California at Los Angeles (UCLA), where Freire's archives are kept.
  • 1997 - Freire died of a heart attack on May 2, 1997, at 6:53 am, at Hospital Albert Einstein in São Paulo.
